The First Car

In the year 1886 Karl Benz a German inventor patented his Benz Patent-Motorwagen.

It is a three wheeled automobile with a rear-mounted engine. It was a single cylinder four-stroke engine. A single belt system served as a single-speed transmission, varying torque between an open disc and drive disc.Steering was by way of a toothed rack that pivoted the unsprung front wheel.

Evolution

As the years passed cars started evolving as the most useful means of transport. They moved from three wheels to four , from two-stroke to four-stroke, from a simple design to complicated design, from a small engine to a sophisticated highly powerful engines. Nowadays cars are known for their speed, luxury and comfort means of road transport.

Evolution Of Engines

The first reliable engine was a two stroke gasoline engine which ran for the first time on 'New Year's Eve' 1879, developed by Karl Benz. Benz had so much commercial success with this engine that he was able to devote more time to his dream of creating a lightweight car powered by a gasoline engine, in which the chassis and engine formed a single unit.

It was in the year 1885, his dreams came true when his two-seater vehicle was mounted with compact high-speed single-cylinder four-stroke engine horizontally at the rear,the tubular steel frame, the differential and three wire-spoked wheels. The engine output was 0.75 hp (0.55 kW). Details included an automatic intake slide, a controlled exhaust valve, high-voltage electrical vibrator ignition with spark plug, and water/thermo siphon evaporation cooling.


Early internal combustion engines were stareted by hand cranking. Today we use electrical starters to ignite our engines. The first piston engines did not have compression, but ran on an air-fuel mixture sucked or blown in during the first part of the intake stroke. The most significant distinction between modern internal combustion engines and the early designs is the use of compression of the fuel charge prior to combustion.
